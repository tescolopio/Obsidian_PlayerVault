# The Dark Forest

The Dark Forest stretches for sixty miles east of Thornwall. Locals avoid it after sunset.

## Terrain

Dense canopy, frequent bogs, and strange magnetic interference make navigation difficult. Without a reliable guide, travellers become lost on a DC 12 Survival failure.

## Encounters

- Giant spiders near the forest edge (common)
- Ruins of a pre-Compact settlement (rare)
- The Watching Trees — ancient treants that observe but seldom speak

%%GM%%
Encounter table (d6, daytime):
1–2. Nothing
3.   1d6 giant wolf spiders
4.   Treant patrol (neutral, will warn the party away from the Rift zone)
5.   Hollow Moon cultist scouting party (3 thugs + 1 mage)
6.   A lost merchant who knows more than he lets on (see [[GM_Session_Notes]])

At night, add +1 to all rolls.
%%GM%%

## The Rift Zone

%%SECRET%%
At the heart of the forest (hex G-14 on the GM map) is a 200-ft clearing of dead trees surrounding a crack in the earth roughly 40 ft long. The crack pulses with faint violet light every 2d6 minutes.

This is the surface manifestation of the sealed planar rift referenced in [[Mayor_Aldric]]'s notes. Getting too close requires a DC 13 Constitution save or the character gains one level of exhaustion per hour.
%%SECRET%%

## Player-Visible Lore

Local legends call the forest *Thornmere* in old Compact-period texts. No comprehensive map of its interior has ever been completed — every surveying expedition has returned incomplete or not at all.
