# The Thornwall Compact

The Thornwall Compact is an ancient merchant coalition that has governed the city's trade routes for over two centuries. #player-facing

%%SECRET%%
The Compact's public role as a trade organisation is a cover. Its inner council controls the city's underworld and has been preparing a succession crisis for three years. The players should not know this until Session 13 at the earliest.
%%SECRET%%

## Public History

The Compact was founded in 198 AE by seven merchant families to standardise weights and measures on the Thornwall–Ironhaven road. Over time it grew to manage:

- Toll collection on all three city gates
- Licensing of the Grand Market stalls
- Arbitration of commercial disputes

## Symbols & Recognition

Members wear a bronze pin shaped like a knotted rope — supposedly symbolising trade ties between cities.

%%GM%%
The rope symbol is actually a coded reference to a hanging. The Compact's founding charter included a non-compete clause enforced by execution. Players with History DC 18 can learn this.
%%GM%%

## Public Standing

The Compact is **respected but not beloved**. Thornwall's merchants tolerate it because disrupting it would collapse the market. Common folk are indifferent.
